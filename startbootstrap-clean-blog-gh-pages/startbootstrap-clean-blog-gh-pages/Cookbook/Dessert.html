4th of July Jello

Dessert Side Dish Snack
Serves: 16 Prep: 6 hrs 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 6 oz red jello mix (1 box)
  • 6 oz blue jello mix (1 box)
  • 14 oz sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 (.25 oz) Knox packets unflavored gelatin
  • Water (boiling and cold)

Directions

  1. Dissolve each flavor of jello in 2 cups boiling water. Pour into two separate 9×13" pans and chill at least 3 hours or overnight.
  2. Cut chilled jello into small squares and mix both colors in one 9×13" pan.
  3. Sprinkle 2 envelopes unflavored gelatin into ½ cup cold water. Let expand a few minutes, then add 1½ cups boiling water and whisk until dissolved.
  4. Mix in sweetened condensed milk; stir and let cool to room temperature.
  5. Pour cooled milk mixture over jello squares. Chill until set, a few hours. Cut into blocks or shapes and serve.

Banana Cream Pie

Dessert Pie
Yield: 1 (9-inch) pie Prep: 30 mins Cook: 12 mins

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup white sugar
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 egg yolks, beaten
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1¼ tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 (9-inch) baked pastry shell, cooled
  • 3 bananas, sliced
  • Whipped Cream: 1 cup cream, ¼ cup powdered sugar, ½ tsp vanilla

Directions

  1. Combine sugar, flour, and salt in a saucepan. Gradually stir in milk. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until bubbly. Cook and stir 2 more minutes; remove from heat.
  2. Stir a small amount of the hot mixture into the beaten egg yolks, then immediately add back to the saucepan. Cook 2 more minutes, stirring constantly.
  3. Remove from heat; add butter and vanilla. Stir until smooth.
  4. Slice bananas into the cooled pastry shell. Pour pudding mixture on top.
  5. Beat cream until thick; add sugar and vanilla and beat until peaks form. Spread over pie. Chill until serving.

Notes

Use 3 bananas.

Best Chocolate Cupcakes

Dessert Cake
Serves: 24 Prep: 10 mins Cook: 20 mins

Ingredients

  • 15 oz box devil's food cake mix
  • 3.9 oz box instant chocolate pudding (dry)
  • 1¼ cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 4 eggs
  • ¾ cup canola oil
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 8 oz sour cream

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F. Line 2 muffin pans with cupcake liners.
  2. Combine cake mix and chocolate pudding. Stir in chocolate chips (this prevents them from sinking). Mix in eggs, canola oil, and water. Stir in sour cream.
  3. Fill liners ¾ full. Bake 20–25 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean. Do not overcook.
  4. Cool completely on a cooling rack before frosting.

Blueberry Cheesecake

Dessert Cake
Serves: 6–8 Prep: 10 mins Cook: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup (50g) butter, melted
  • 20 oz (600g) cream cheese
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup (175g) caster sugar
  • 2 eggs + 1 yolk
  • Small pot soured cream
  • Vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (300g) fresh or frozen blueberries
  • Icing sugar

Directions

  1. Mix melted butter into cracker crumbs. Press into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake at 325°F for 10–12 minutes until set. Allow to cool.
  2. Beat cream cheese with flour, caster sugar, eggs, soured cream, and vanilla until light and fluffy.
  3. Pour into pan and bake 35–40 minutes until set. Remove and let cool.
  4. Heat half the blueberries with 2 tbsp icing sugar, stirring gently until juicy. Squash with a fork and cook a few more minutes. Add remaining blueberries, remove from heat, and cool.
  5. Pour blueberries over cheesecake just before serving.

Camping S'mores Dutch Oven Brownies

Dessert Cookies/Bars
Serves: 8 Prep: 5 mins Cook: 30 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 (18 oz) box brownie mix, prepared per package directions
  • ½ cup chopped pecans
  • 6 large rectangular graham cracker sheets, broken into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups mini marshmallows

Directions

  1. Preheat 10" Dutch oven to 350°F (21 total coals = 14 top / 7 bottom).
  2. Mix marshmallows and graham cracker pieces in a bowl.
  3. Prepare brownie mix per package directions; stir in pecans.
  4. Line Dutch oven with parchment paper. Pour in brownie batter, cover, and bake ~15 minutes (par-bake).
  5. Remove lid, sprinkle marshmallow/graham mixture over top, re-cover, and bake 15–20 more minutes until brownies are done and marshmallows are toasted.
  6. Lift out using parchment edges. Cool slightly and serve warm.

Chocolate Buttercream Frosting

Dessert Cake
Frosts: 24 cupcakes Prep: 10 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 4–5 cups powdered sugar
  • 5–6 tbsp natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3 tbsp half-and-half, heavy cream, or evaporated milk
  • 1½ tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Directions

  1. Beat butter for 30 seconds with an electric mixer.
  2. Mix in 1 cup powdered sugar, cocoa, 1 tbsp half-and-half, and vanilla on medium speed until smooth.
  3. Add another cup of powdered sugar and 1 tbsp half-and-half; beat until smooth. Repeat until all 4 cups of sugar are incorporated.
  4. Beat until smooth and creamy. Taste; add more salt or vanilla if needed.

Chocolate Molten Lava Cakes

Dessert Cake
Serves: 8 (½ cake each) Prep: 15 mins Cook: 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 pkg (4 oz) Baker's Semi-Sweet Chocolate
  • ½ cup butter
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 whole eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 6 tbsp flour
  • ½ cup Cool Whip for serving

Directions

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Butter 4 small custard cups and place on a baking sheet.
  2. Microwave chocolate and butter on HIGH 1 minute or until butter is melted; whisk until chocolate is completely melted.
  3. Stir in powdered sugar. Add eggs and yolks; mix well. Stir in flour. Spoon into prepared cups.
  4. Bake 13–14 minutes until edges are firm but centers are still soft. Let stand 1 minute.
  5. Run a knife around cakes to loosen; invert onto dessert plates. Serve warm with Cool Whip.

Muffin Tin Variation

Pour into 10 greased/floured muffin cups. Bake 6–7 minutes until sides are firm but centers are still soft.

Cookies and Cream Dessert Cups

Dessert
Serves: 28 (2-oz cups) Prep: 30 mins

Ingredients

  • 2 cups crushed chocolate sandwich cookies
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 8 oz cream cheese, room temperature
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1½ cups heavy cream
  • ½ cup crushed sandwich cookies (for filling)
  • 28 mini chocolate sandwich cookies (for topping)

Directions

  1. Combine cookie crumbs and melted butter. Portion into 28 (2-oz) cups; press into bottom of each cup.
  2. Beat cream cheese and sugar together until smooth.
  3. Add heavy cream and vanilla; whip on medium-high until light and fluffy. Fold in crushed cookie crumbs.
  4. Pipe mixture into cups until ¾ full. Top each with a mini cookie or extra crumbs.
  5. Seal and chill. Serve immediately or refrigerate up to 2–3 days.

Creamy Chocolate Frosting

Dessert
Frosts: 1 cake Prep: 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 2¾ cups confectioners' sugar
  • 6 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 6 tbsp butter
  • 5 tbsp evaporated milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions

  1. Sift together confectioners' sugar and cocoa; set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, cream butter until smooth. Gradually beat in sugar mixture alternately with evaporated milk.
  3. Blend in vanilla. Beat until light and fluffy. Adjust consistency with more milk or sugar as needed.

Easy Homemade Vanilla Cupcakes

Dessert Cake
Serves: 12 Prep: 1 hour Cook: 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cups (163g) all-purpose flour
  • 1¼ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 6 tbsp (84g) unsalted butter, room temperature
  • ¾ cup (155g) sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup + 2 tbsp (150ml) milk
  • Frosting: 1 cup (224g) unsalted butter · 4 cups (460g) powdered sugar · 1½ tsp vanilla · 2–3 tbsp milk · pinch salt

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line cupcake pan with liners.
  2. Whisk flour, baking powder, and salt; set aside.
  3. Beat butter, sugar, oil, and vanilla until light and fluffy, 3–4 minutes.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, mixing after each.
  5. Add half the dry ingredients; mix. Slowly add milk; mix. Add remaining dry ingredients and mix until smooth. Do not over-mix.
  6. Fill liners ¾ full. Bake 15–18 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean. Cool completely.
  7. Frosting: Beat butter until smooth. Add half the powdered sugar; mix. Add vanilla, 1–2 tbsp milk, and salt. Add remaining sugar; mix until smooth, adding milk as needed. Pipe onto cooled cupcakes.

Easy Pecan Pie

Dessert Pie
Serves: 8 Prep: 10 mins Cook: 40 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 (9-inch) deep-dish frozen pie crust, thawed
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • ⅓ cup butter, melted
  • ¾ cup corn syrup
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 cups pecan halves, divided
  • Whipped cream (optional)

Directions

  1. Thaw pie crust. Place on a baking sheet. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Whisk together brown sugar, melted butter, corn syrup, salt, and eggs.
  3. Coarsely chop 1½ cups pecans; fold into filling. Pour into pie crust. Top with remaining pecan halves.
  4. Bake 35–40 minutes until center is set.
  5. Cool and refrigerate before serving. Top with whipped cream if desired.

Notes

Cover crust edges with foil to prevent burning. Cover entire pie with foil after 30 minutes. Cook until center reaches 200°F — check at 40 minutes.

Fudgy Chocolate Cream Pie

Dessert Pie
Yield: 1 (9-inch) pie Prep: 20 mins Cook: 25 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 (9-inch) pie crust, baked
  • 1¼ cups white sugar
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1½ cups milk
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1.5–2 oz unsweetened chocolate, chopped
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Whipped Cream: 1 cup cream, ¼ cup powdered sugar, ½ tsp vanilla

Directions

  1. Whisk together sugar, flour, cornstarch, and salt in a saucepan. Whisk milk and egg yolks in a bowl; gradually stir into sugar mixture. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened and at a full boil. Boil and stir 1 minute.
  2. Remove from heat; stir in chocolate, butter, and vanilla until melted and smooth.
  3. Pour into pie shell. Place plastic wrap directly on filling to prevent a skin. Chill several hours.
  4. Beat cream until thick; add sugar and vanilla and beat to peaks. Spread over chilled pie.

Lemon Tart

Dessert Tart
Serves: 8 Prep: 1 hour Cook: 30 mins

Ingredients

  • Tart Shell:
  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup icing sugar
  • ⅔ cup softened butter
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • Lemon Curd:
  • 6 lemons (zest and juice)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1½ cups caster sugar
  • 1½ cups cream

Directions

  1. Mix butter and icing sugar; beat in egg yolk. Add flour and salt; rub together until crumbly. Knead briefly into a firm dough. Wrap and chill 30 minutes.
  2. Roll pastry very thin and line a quiche tin, letting a little overlap the edges. Prick base with a fork and bake at 400°F for 20 minutes.
  3. Grate lemon zest into a bowl and add the juice. Whisk eggs and caster sugar; add lemon and cream, whisking gently.
  4. Pour into pastry case and bake at 350°F for 25–30 minutes until set.
  5. Sprinkle with icing sugar and serve with cream.

Peanut Butter Chocolate Bars

Dessert Snack Cookies/Bars
Yield: 2 dozen Prep: 30 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 6 cups crisp rice cereal
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips, melted

Directions

  1. Combine sugar, corn syrup, and peanut butter in a large saucepan. Cook and stir over medium-low heat until sugar is dissolved.

White Chocolate Lasagna

Dessert
Serves: 12 Prep: 10 mins + 1 hr chill

Ingredients

  • 1 package Golden Oreos (36 cookies)
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup margarine or butter
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 16 oz Cool Whip, divided
  • 7.8 oz white chocolate (or vanilla) instant pudding mix
  • 3 cups milk
  • White chocolate bar (for curls)

Directions

  1. Crush Oreos finely. Mix with melted butter until combined. Press into the bottom of a 9×13 pan.
  2. Blend cream cheese, margarine, powdered sugar, and half the Cool Whip. Spread over crust.
  3. Whisk pudding mix with milk until thickened. Pour over cream cheese layer. Refrigerate 5 minutes.
  4. Spread remaining Cool Whip on top. Add white chocolate shavings.
  5. Refrigerate at least 1 hour before serving.
