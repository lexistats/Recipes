Biographical Sketch

When I was younger, I would walk into a room and see my brother and sister “playing library” in a cozy laundry basket with blankets surrounding them, engrossed in a book. Watching them, I could not wait to start reading. Books fostered a love for adventurous worlds and boundless possibilities of imagination. I often found myself immersed in playing make-believe games and casting myself as a character with a captivating tale. Whenever I am curious about an idea, I can look it up and learn more about how I can apply it to my life. This has allowed me to learn more about the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints and the desire to attend BYU. Reading was the spark for my creative journey.

As my siblings transitioned into Jr. High, they became involved in the school band. Watching my sister march proudly down the streets for a parade filled me with excitement to participate in this activity as I could tell she was enjoying herself. I wanted to be just like her, so I picked up a flute and I started learning how to play. I recall hearing her play a beautiful solo, motivating me to make it a mission to play as well as her someday. Pursuing this cultivated my creativity as I learned effective practice strategies to achieve this goal.

Whether listening to instrumental or pop music with my siblings, I often found myself envisioning other glorious worlds. This vision is what propelled me to do the activities I have done in my life. In my mind, an aerialist climbed at the crescendo of a song and then suddenly dropped into a series of fast twists as the music hit the climax. I am now in the aerial silks class at BYU so I can learn how to do this. I would see teams rallied together, driven by a determination to accomplish their goals. Before every marching band competition, I would try to get my teammates excited to perform their best. Sometimes I would envision a dance team performing beautiful movements together. Seeing something breathtaking gave me the ability to choreograph dances throughout my life, whether solo or team. Books and music became the cornerstones of my creative expression, thanks to the influence of my siblings.

I am fortunate to have been born into a supportive family that has made so many things possible for me I would not have otherwise known. Their encouragement pushed me to do things I could never have seen myself doing. My family instilled a sense of worth in me and the belief that I can achieve greatness through unwavering determination. I felt excluded many times at school when I was younger: and like I somehow did not fit in. However, high school provided transformative experiences when I was able to be a leader in band. I made it a mission to always include others, ensuring that no one felt the way I once did.
